Octopuses have three hearts

FACT

Octopuses truly have three hearts

Octopuses possess three hearts: two branchial hearts and one systemic heart. The two branchial hearts pump blood through the gills, where it gets oxygenated, while the systemic heart circulates the oxygenated blood to the rest of the body.

Interestingly, the systemic heart temporarily stops beating when the octopus swims, which is why they often prefer crawling to conserve energy. This unique circulatory system is part of what makes octopuses so fascinating.
