Bananas grow on trees

FICTION

Bananas don't grow on trees, they grow on large herbs

Although they may look like trees, banana plants are technically giant herbs. Their "trunk" is actually a pseudostem made of tightly packed leaf bases, not wood.

The banana plant is classified as an herb because it does not have a woody structure. The bananas themselves are berries by botanical definition, growing in clusters on these towering herbaceous plants.
