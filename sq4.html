Cracking your knuckles causes arthritis

FICTION

Cracking your knuckles does NOT cause arthritis

Studies have found no evidence linking knuckle cracking to arthritis. The sound you hear when cracking your knuckles is caused by bubbles of gas collapsing or bursting within the joint fluid, not by damaging the joints.

While habitual knuckle cracking may lead to some minor swelling or reduced grip strength in rare cases, it is not associated with the development of arthritis.

Arthritis primarily results from factors such as genetics, age, and injury, not knuckle cracking.
